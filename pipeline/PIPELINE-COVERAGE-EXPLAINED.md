# Pipeline Coverage vs Revenue Gap — Explained

## 🎯 The Core Concept

**Pipeline Coverage** measures whether you have **enough deals in your pipeline** to hit your revenue targets, accounting for the fact that not all deals will close.

## 📊 The Three Key Numbers

### 1. **Revenue Gap to Budget** ($3.8M in the example)

This is the **shortfall** between where you are today and where you need to be by year-end.

**Example scenario:**
- Annual revenue target: $120M
- YTD actual revenue: $58M (as of June)
- Year-end forecast: $116.2M
- **Gap: $3.8M** — the additional revenue needed to hit target

This gap exists because the region is running **behind plan**. The pipeline needs to generate enough new revenue to close this gap.

### 2. **Weighted Pipeline** ($3.04M in the example)

This is the **probability-adjusted value** of all open deals in the pipeline.

**How it's calculated:**
```
For each deal:
  Weighted Revenue = Annual Revenue × Probability

Example deals:
- Deal A: $500K revenue, 80% probability → $400K weighted
- Deal B: $200K revenue, 25% probability → $50K weighted
- Deal C: $1M revenue, 50% probability → $500K weighted

Total Weighted Pipeline = $950K
```

**Why weight by probability?**
- A Lead-stage deal (10% probability) is less certain than a Commit-stage deal (80% probability)
- Weighting gives you a **realistic estimate** of how much revenue you'll actually get from the pipeline
- It's the "expected value" if the probability estimates are accurate

### 3. **Coverage Ratio** (0.80x in the example)

This is the **multiple** of how many times your weighted pipeline covers the revenue gap.

**Formula:**
```
Coverage Ratio = Weighted Pipeline ÷ Revenue Gap

Example:
$3.04M ÷ $3.8M = 0.80x
```

## 🚦 What the Coverage Ratio Means

### ✅ **Green: ≥ 3.0x** (Healthy)
- You have **3× or more** weighted pipeline relative to the gap
- Even if only 1 out of 3 deals closes, you'll hit your target
- This is a **best practice** cushion that banks typically want

**Example:** If you need $3.8M and have $11.4M weighted pipeline
- Coverage = 11.4 ÷ 3.8 = **3.0x**
- Gives room for deals to fall through, slip, or come in smaller than expected

### ⚠️ **Amber: 1.5x - 3.0x** (Caution)
- You have **some cushion** but not the recommended amount
- If deals underperform or slip to next period, you'll miss target
- Should be **actively building pipeline**

**Example:** If you need $3.8M and have $5.7M weighted pipeline
- Coverage = 5.7 ÷ 3.8 = **1.5x**
- Only a 50% buffer — risky

### 🔴 **Red: < 1.5x** (At Risk)
- **Insufficient pipeline** to hit target
- Even if deals perform as expected, you'll fall short
- **Critical need** for new pipeline generation

**Example (current state):** Need $3.8M, have $3.04M weighted pipeline
- Coverage = 3.04 ÷ 3.8 = **0.80x**
- Only 80% of what you need — **will miss target** unless:
  - New deals are added to pipeline
  - Existing deals are advanced to higher-probability stages
  - Deal sizes increase

## 📈 Why 3x Coverage is the Standard

The **3x rule** accounts for three realities of pipeline management:

1. **Probability Overestimation** (30-40% typical)
   - RMs often overestimate deal probability
   - "50% chance" deals might really be 35% likely
   - 3x cushion compensates for optimism bias

2. **Deal Slippage** (20-30% of deals)
   - Deals push to next quarter
   - Decision timelines extend
   - These deals don't contribute to current period revenue

3. **Deal Attrition** (10-20% of deals)
   - Competitive losses
   - Customer funding falls through
   - Deal size shrinks
   - Some deals just die

**With 3x coverage:**
- If you need $3.8M in revenue
- You carry $11.4M weighted pipeline (3 × $3.8M)
- Even with 40% optimism bias + 25% slippage + 15% attrition
- You still have enough deals to close the gap

## 💡 How to Improve Coverage

If your coverage ratio is low (like the current 0.80x), you have three levers:

### 1. **Add New Deals to Pipeline**
- Prospect for new opportunities
- Source deals from marketing campaigns
- Reactivate dormant prospects
- **Impact:** Increases both total and weighted pipeline

### 2. **Advance Existing Deals**
- Move deals from Lead (10%) → Qualified (25%) → Proposal (50%)
- Build stronger relationships, address objections
- Get to Commit stage (80% probability)
- **Impact:** Increases weighted pipeline without adding deals

### 3. **Increase Deal Sizes**
- Expand scope of engagement (cross-sell)
- Include additional products (loan + TM + deposits)
- Negotiate larger commitments
- **Impact:** Increases revenue per deal

## 🎓 Real-World Example

**Scenario:** Houston market needs to close a $2.1M revenue gap

**Current pipeline:**
- 35 open deals
- $127M total balance
- $5.8M total annual revenue (unweighted)
- $1.5M weighted annual revenue (after probability adjustment)

**Analysis:**
```
Coverage Ratio = $1.5M ÷ $2.1M = 0.71x (RED)
```

**What this means:**
- Even if every deal performs exactly as expected, Houston will generate $1.5M
- They'll still be **$600K short** of the $2.1M gap
- Houston needs to **immediately**:
  - Add $4.8M in new weighted pipeline to reach 3x coverage
  - Or move existing deals to higher stages (Lead → Qualified → Proposal)
  - Or increase deal sizes / scope

**Leadership action:**
- Assign Houston market additional prospecting resources
- Review stuck deals (>90 days in stage) and create action plans
- Consider reallocating deals from Dallas if they're over-covered

## 📝 Key Takeaways

1. **Revenue Gap** = How much additional revenue you need to hit target
2. **Weighted Pipeline** = Realistic expectation of revenue from current deals (probability-adjusted)
3. **Coverage Ratio** = Weighted Pipeline ÷ Revenue Gap
4. **Target is 3.0x** to account for optimism, slippage, and attrition
5. **Below 1.5x is critical** — immediate pipeline-building action needed
6. The dashboard tracks this at **region, market, and officer levels**

---

This metric is the **single most important** pipeline health indicator because it tells you whether you're on track to hit revenue targets **before** it's too late to course-correct.
